Theory Guide: Natural Selection v3.0

1. Variation (Phenotype):
Every blob has unique traits: Speed, Size, and Sense Radius. In 'Traits' view, Red blobs are fast/agile, Blue are slow/efficient. Predators appear with a dark red ring.

2. Inheritance (Genotype):
When a blob eats enough to replicate, it passes its 'Genes' to offspring with slight random mutations. This introduces variation into the gene pool, including predatory traits.

3. Selection Pressure:
Energy is life. Moving fast and being big burns energy rapidly. Blobs must eat to survive. Predators have higher metabolic costs. If they run out of energy, they die. This "culls" inefficient designs.

4. Predator-Prey Dynamics (NEW):
The ecosystem includes both herbivores (eating plants/food) and carnivores (hunting herbivores). Watch how populations oscillate as predators are limited by prey availability, and herbivores rebound when predator populations decline.

5. Ecosystem Health:
The status indicator shows the ecosystem balance. Critical warnings appear when populations become dangerously imbalanced. Total extinction forces a reset.

6. Speciation:
If an offspring is genetically distinct enough (>20% difference) from its parent, it receives a unique Latin name representing a new species.
