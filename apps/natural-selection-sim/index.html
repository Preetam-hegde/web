Theory Guide: Natural Selection

1. Variation (Phenotype):
Every blob has unique traits: Speed, Size, and Sense Radius. In 'Traits' view, Red blobs are fast/agile, Blue are slow/efficient.

2. Inheritance (Genotype):
When a blob eats enough to replicate, it passes its 'Genes' to offspring with slight random mutations. This introduces variation into the gene pool.

3. Selection Pressure:
Energy is life. Moving fast and being big burns energy rapidly. Blobs must eat to survive. If they run out of energy, they die. This "culls" inefficient designs.

4. Speciation:
If an offspring is genetically distinct enough (>20% difference) from its parent, it is classified as a new species with a unique Latin name.
